About

Studio & Mission

We reclaim Indonesian industrial material waste and turn it into interior surfaces that designers actually want to specify.

Founding Story

Why does good
material get wasted?

Reka Ruang was founded by designers who kept watching high-quality material leave Indonesian factories and construction sites as waste. Broken ceramic stock. Glass fragments. Mineral aggregate. Material with form, character, and physical integrity — discarded because it was no longer suitable in its original format.

The studio exists to answer a different question: if this material still has value, can we turn it into something a specifier would proudly put on a project drawing?

Vision & Mission

Vision

Mission

Upcycling Process

How waste becomes
interior material.

Our production model is circular. We source waste material from local Indonesian factories, demolition sites, and material suppliers — collected in the state they arrive and processed at our studio into tile-grade material.

01

Sourcing

Broken ceramics, glass fragments, mineral aggregate, and fired clay waste collected from industrial partners and demolition sites across Indonesia.

02

Processing

Materials are cleaned, sorted by type and visual character, then crushed, milled, or sized depending on the tile variant being produced.

03

Composing

Processed aggregate is blended with a minimal binder — cement, eco-resin, or hydraulic material — and formed into tiles using our pressing method.

04

Curing & QC

Tiles cure for a minimum of 28 days, then pass dimensional and surface quality checks before release for specification.
